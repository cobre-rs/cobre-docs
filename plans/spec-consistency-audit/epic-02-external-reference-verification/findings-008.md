### Contextual Issues (Detailed Discussion)

#### Issue 1: cut-management.md section 8 -- Guigues & Bandarra convergence claim (CORRECT)

**Claim**: "Under Level-1 or LML1 cut selection, SDDP with finitely many scenarios converges to the optimal value function with probability 1."

**Verification**: Guigues & Bandarra (2019, arXiv:1902.06757; published 2021 in _Computational Management Science_) prove:

- Theorem 3.1: Convergence of single-cut SDDP with Level-1 cut selection
- Theorem 4.1: Convergence of multi-cut SDDP with Level-1 cut selection
- Section 5: Extension to LML1 (Limited Memory Level-1)
- The convergence is finite (with probability 1) under the assumption of finitely many realizations per stage

The claim in the spec accurately summarizes the paper's result. The "finitely many scenarios" qualification is correct and important -- the result does not apply to continuous distributions.

**Verdict**: CORRECT. This is the most important citation to verify per the ticket instructions, and it checks out.

#### Issue 2: Costa et al. (2025) -- Wrong paper at DOI

**Claim in infinite-horizon.md**: The spec attributes the cyclic policy graph and infinite periodic horizon formulation to "Costa, B.S., de Matos, V.L., Philpott, A.B. (2025). SDDP.jl approaches for infinite horizon problems."

**Problem**: The DOI resolves to a completely different paper by different authors with a different title. This makes the citation contextually unverifiable. The formulation in the spec (cyclic policy graphs, seasonal cut sharing, fixed-point interpretation) is mathematically sound and consistent with known SDDP.jl infinite horizon approaches, but the attribution cannot be confirmed.

**Impact**: HIGH. The spec's primary reference for its core formulation is broken.

#### Issue 3: Costa & Leclere (2023) -- Wrong title at URL

**Claim in upper-bound-evaluation.md**: The spec attributes the Lipschitz-based vertex inner approximation (SIDP) to "Costa, B.S., & Leclere, V. (2023). Lipschitz-based Inner Approximation of Risk Measures."

**Problem**: The URL points to "Duality of upper bounds in stochastic dynamic programming." The spec describes a specific technique (Lipschitz interpolation, vertex-based inner approximation) and attributes it to this paper, but the actual paper at the URL has a different focus. The technique described in the spec may come from a different paper by the same authors (possibly arXiv:2107.10930 or the 2024 journal version).

**Impact**: HIGH. The spec's primary reference for its core technique has wrong metadata.

#### Issue 4: Larroyd et al. (2022) -- Wrong title and authors for DOI

**Claim in inflow-nonnegativity.md**: References the paper for inflow non-negativity methods.

**Problem**: The DOI resolves to a paper about negative inflows by different authors. The topic matches but the metadata does not. The spec's claim about inflow non-negativity treatment is likely correct for the actual paper at the DOI, but the citation gives the wrong title and wrong co-authors.

**Impact**: MEDIUM. The contextual claim is probably correct, but the metadata mismatch creates confusion.

#### Issue 5: Philpott et al. (2013) -- Wrong DOI propagated to specs

**Problem**: The bibliography has DOI `10.1287/opre.2013.1200` which resolves to Bertsimas et al. (a completely different paper). This wrong DOI is propagated to `risk-measures.md` (line 270) and `upper-bound-evaluation.md` (line 209). Any reader clicking the DOI link will reach the wrong paper.

**Impact**: HIGH. Two spec files contain a clickable DOI link to the wrong paper.

#### Issue 6: de Matos et al. (2015) -- Orphan despite being foundational

**Problem**: The bibliography claims this paper is "Basis for the cut selection in `cobre-sddp`", yet it is never cited in any spec or algorithm file. The cut management spec and cut selection algorithm page cite only Guigues & Bandarra (2019) for convergence, with no mention of de Matos et al. (2015) as the source of the Level-1 strategy itself.

**Impact**: LOW. Not a correctness issue, but a completeness gap. The Level-1 cut selection strategy was proposed in de Matos et al. (2015) and should be cited in `cut-management.md` section 7.1.
